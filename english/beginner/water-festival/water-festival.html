Nhi & the Water Festival

🎧 Listen to the text

Nhi is a young girl from Vietnam. She lives in Ho Chi Minh City but her home town is in Dong Nai province. One day, she hears about a big water festival in Thailand. The festival is called Songkran. It is the Thai New Year. People celebrate it every year in April.

Long ago, Songkran was a quiet holiday. People cleaned their homes and visited family. They also poured water on Buddha statues and on older people to show respect and wish for good luck. Water means washing away bad things from the old year. Now, the festival is also a fight in the streets!

Nhi is very excited. She takes a plane to Thailand. When she arrives, she sees many people with water guns and buckets. Everyone is smiling and laughing. The weather is hot, so the water feels cool and nice.

Nhi buys some water balloons. She fills them with water and throws them at people. Splash! A boy throws one at her too. She laughs and runs. Soon, she is very wet, but very happy. She plays all day in the streets.

Later, Nhi feels hungry. She tries Thai food at a small restaurant. She eats pad thai and spicy soup. The food is very tasty, but also very spicy. "Oh! Too hot!" she says, but she keeps eating and smiles.

Nhi meets a nice Thai boy named Arun. He smiles and says, "Hello!" Nhi says hello too. Arun does not speak Vietnamese, and Nhi does not speak Thai. So they try to talk in English. Their English is not perfect, but they laugh and understand a little. They point at things, use simple words, and make jokes. They play with water together and take photos. Nhi feels happy to meet a new friend from another country.

At the end of her trip, Nhi thinks about Thailand. She likes the kind people, the fun festival, and the rich culture. She says, "I want to come back next year!"

Nhi goes home to Vietnam with great memories, new experiences, and a big smile.
