Episode 4: Secrets and Strong Coffee

🎧 Listen to the text

Maya and Jack climbed the balcony just in time. They scrambled over the railing and hid in the shadows as Thorne's men searched the alley below. After the men finally left, he led Maya through a hidden door into a small, secret apartment.

The room smelled of roasted coffee and old paper. Maps were pinned to every wall.

"Welcome to my hideout," Jack said, finally taking off his jacket. He looked tired, but his smile was still warm.

"I need answers, Jack," Maya said, her voice firm. "You're not just a photographer. You have a weapon, you knew my grandfather, and you're being chased by the same people as me."

Jack sighed and walked to a small kitchen area. He began to make traditional Vietnamese coffee. "You're right. I work for a group that protects cultural heritage. We try to stop people like Marcus Thorne from stealing a country's history."

He handed Maya a glass of coffee. It was thick, dark, and very sweet. "Your grandfather was our best agent. Years ago, he hid the Jade Dragon to keep it safe from Thorne's cabal."

Maya took a sip of the coffee. The caffeine made her feel more alert. She looked at the golden key on the table. "And this key?"

"It opens a box in a hidden temple in Đà Lạt," he explained. "But we have a problem. The map in your journal is encoded. We can't read the directions without a special lens."

Maya smiled, a clever spark in her eyes. She reached into her bag and pulled out a small, round piece of glass. "You mean this? My grandfather gave it to me as a 'lucky charm' when I was a little girl."

Jack stared at the glass, then at Maya. "You're incredible, Maya. You've had the missing piece this whole time!"

For a moment, they stood very close to each other. The tension in the room wasn't just about the mystery anymore. Maya felt her heart race as Jack reached out to touch the glass.

But at that moment, a red laser dot appeared on the wall behind them. It was moving rapidly toward Jack's chest. Someone was on the roof across the street with a sniper rifle!

"Get down, Jack!" Maya screamed.

A bullet shattered the coffee glass in Maya's hand.
